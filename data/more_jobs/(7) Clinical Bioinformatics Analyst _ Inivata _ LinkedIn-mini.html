About the job

This position offers the exciting opportunity to work at the frontier of cancer diagnostics. The successful candidate will work within the Clinical Team, with a primary focus on processing and analysing next-generation sequencing data from clinical cohorts and trials. They will ensure that the analysis of sequencing runs is performed in a timely and reliable manner, monitoring the clinical data through a series of metrics to identify trends early and correct course when appropriate.


This will require assisting with troubleshooting production pipeline issues and debugging code. As such, this role will involve close collaboration with the Computation Biology Group to ensure on-time delivery of samples. Additionally, this role will provide analytical support in the preparation of posters and manuscripts by writing or modifying existing scripts to produce custom plots and reports.

We expect that applicants will have prior experience in the field of bioinformatics and a solid understanding of NGS analysis, for example a MSc or BSc degree in a relevant field or prior industry experience.


Education/Qualifications:

Technical Requirement:

  • MSc or BSc in the field of computational biology/bioinformatics, or prior industry experience in a relevant field.


Skill requirement:

  • Scientific programming experience in R and/or Python (Essential)
  • Familiarity with data visualisation libraries in R/Python such as ggplot2 or matplotlib (Essential)
  • Experience working in a UNIX environment (Essential)
  • Experience with or strong desire to learn next generation sequence data analysis (Essential)
  • Experience with cancer genomics data (Desired)
  • Experience with variant calling and exome/WGS pipelines (Desired)
  • Prior experience with work in a regulated environment, in particular for software and quality assurance, such as ISO 13485, EN 62304, ISO 15189, or other (Desired)
  • Excellent communication and presentation skills
