About the job

Data Scientist/Economist - Data Science

Software Development | United States | Remote

$100,000-120,000 base salary + equity + benefits


A mission-oriented software development company is building a unique talent-matching product that is powered by machine learning. To help lead the initiative, they are in need of an outstanding Research Data Scientist with experience in labor economics. You will work closely with the Chief Data Scientist and take on a fascinating technical problem in making human talent legible.


THE ROLE

  • Improve existing models and develop new models to analyze and describe how talent moves through the economy
  • Manage and run experiments with a mix of public and private data sources
  • Produce rigorous documentation of findings, including white papers and/or peer-reviewed publications as appropriate
  • Formulate new, adjacent research questions and train models to address them


YOUR SKILLS AND EXPERIENCE

A successful Data Scientist/Economist will likely have the following skills and experience:


  • A track record of novel research using machine learning techniques to answer scientific questions (experience with algorithmic bias and labor economics is a major bonus)
  • Advanced proficiency in machine learning tools and techniques (e.g. time series analysis methods)
  • Knowledge of dimensionality reduction techniques (e.g. PCA, SVD, t-SNE, UMAP, etc.)
  • Knowledge of web frameworks for API development (e.g. Flask)
  • Technical skillset in Python (data science libraries), PySpark, Spark, and SageMaker
  • A creative and resourceful problem solver
  • An excellent written communicator
  • Experience in the talent, workforce, and/or education sectors


THE BENEFITS

A competitive base salary of $100,000-$120,000 + equity + benefits.
