About the job

Company Description

DataPoint Armenia is a non-profit organization created by a group of students and professionals passionate about data science. We have identified an existing need for data science literacy and utilization in Armenia, and our aim is to expand awareness, collaboration, and competency in the field to instill a culture of evidence-based decision-making.


Our network consists of driven individuals and eclectic resources that aim to engage and support professionals and enthusiasts in the field of data science. Through concentrating talent, information, and activity, the network provides an environment where members can effectively pursue their career and academic interests while driving the Armenian data science revolution.

Project Description

It is an unfortunate fact that, more often than not, when Armenian is =E2=80=9Cspoken=E2=80=9D online, it is written in Latin script. That is, instead of using the Armenian alphabet to write something like =E2=80=9C=D4=B2=D5=A1=D6=80=D5=A5=D6=82, =D5=AB=D5=B6=D5=B9=D5=BA=D5=A5=D5=BD =D5=A5=D6=84,=E2=80=9D instead, it is transliterated to =E2=80=9Cbarev/parev, inchbes ek.=E2=80=9D We believe that an Armenian Spell Checker is the solution to this issue. This project will aim to address the main issue that has led to this trend. If this problem is resolved, more people will begin to write in Armenian on their computers, thus creating a larger online Armenian presence. This will not only help give the Armenian online community a more serious look and feel, but it will also give the diaspora a chance to practice reading and writing in Armenian non-phonetically. We have a beautiful and unique alphabet, with a long and interesting history. It is not only a shame to not put it to use, but it could prove to be dangerous if the youth of the current generation, the first to have mainstream internet, doesn=E2=80=99t begin the practice of using it in an everyday setting, especially in the most used medium. The main goal of this project is to increase the ease of use of the Armenian alphabet in the digital realm. Particularly for users who tend to use latin letters to type Armenian or fall back to another language due to lack of spellcheck & autocorrect tools.
